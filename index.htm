Life

Just a small town boy, living in a loooonely world! Just kidding. Born and raised and still living in the suburbs of DC. My wife Nicole, the girl I took to my senior prom; just had our first child, Samantha. We also have a dog, Lola, and cat, Angie. I went to college at Virginia Wesleyan located in VA Beach, where I majored in Business. I played basketball through college developing a work ethic, growing up in the sport. More

I would say I am a people person, but am also independent, often waking up early while the house is quiet, so I can recharge. I take this time to study and read fiction, a passion of mine. I have always respected people with certain skills, not Liam Neeson, but painters, carpenters, architects, writers, etc. This is why identify with Web Development, learning a skill I can use to create.

Work

I was a Mortgage Loan Processor for seven years. It taught me multitasking, how to meet deadlines, attention to detail and gave me office experience. I was good at this position, but intrinsically unfulfilled. I am happiest when I can indulge my curiosity, have a change of scenery and work on something that can produce a tangible result. More

Numbers I plug into a spreadsheet at the end of the month before starting over again doesn't do it for me. This was something I also experienced as a Merchandiser for a wine distributor, my venture after loan processing. I enjoyed the people I worked with, but it left me rudderless and with a sore back. It was also something I jumped into too soon, happy for the change. I think with my new career path I have tested the waters sufficiently and am excited to get started. More importantly I am excited to build, to give someone a product of my labor, a skill I took the time to learn, that they can use to their benefit. I find that fulfilling.

Web

I looked into Web Development at the suggestion of a friend. From there I took a Certificate course at George Mason University for Web Design and Development, which I obtained in September, and since, have been studying on my own, exploring different subjects in the field. I found that I enjoy the coding aspect, looking behind the scenes and learning how webpages and apps have come to life. More

I have also learned, doing it on your own, through books and online tutorials is difficult, and I need the push of my peers and the classroom to focus and give myself direction. I can let my curiosity get the better of me, letting myself get off task, trying to learn something new instead of working on the project I was on. Being in the classroom, having an objective or common goal will nullify that. I believe I work better with someone looking over on my shoulder. Just a little someone.
